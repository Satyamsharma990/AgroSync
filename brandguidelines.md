# Brand Identity Guidelines: KrishiSetu (AgroSync)

## 1. Brand Essence & Mission
- **The Mission:** To democratize agricultural intelligence for the "Bottom of the Pyramid" by turning scattered data into harvestable profits.
- **The Personality:** 
  - Empathetic: Understands the struggle of the small-scale farmer.
  - Scientific: Built on hard data (Soil, Weather, Mandis).
  - Simple: Removes the "tech barrier" through voice and local language.
  - Protective: Shields farmers from middlemen and market volatility.

## 2. Visual Identity Strategy
### A. Color Palette
The colors should bridge the gap between "Tech" and "Nature."
| Element | Color Name | Hex Code | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Primary | Harvest Gold | `#F2B705` | Represents ripening crops, prosperity, and success. |
| Secondary | Earth Green | `#2D592C` | Represents growth, sustainability, and the field. |
| Accent | Tech Blue | `#1A73E8` | Represents the AI, data, and digital reliability. |
| Neutral | Soil Brown | `#4B3621` | Used for grounding elements and text. |

### B. Typography
- **Headings:** Inter or Roboto (Bold). Clean, modern, and highly legible even on low-resolution screens.
- **Body Text:** Open Sans. Optimized for readability in long-form government scheme descriptions.
- **Local Language Support:** Must prioritize fonts that render perfectly in Hindi, Punjabi, Tamil, etc. (e.g., Noto Sans).

## 3. UI/UX Design Principles for Farmers
To ensure the web app is actually used, Antigravity should follow these UX pillars:
- **Icon-First Navigation:** Since literacy levels vary, every major action (Sell, Plant, Weather) must have a distinct, culturally relevant icon.
- **The "Big Button" Rule:** Farmers often use phones in the field with sunlight glare or dirty hands. Buttons should be large, high-contrast, and easy to tap.
- **Voice-Centric Design:** Integration of a "Mic" icon on every page to allow the user to ask questions instead of typing.
- **Low-Bandwidth Optimization:** Use SVG icons and compressed assets to ensure the app loads on 3G/Low-4G connections.

## 4. Tone of Voice (Copywriting)
| Instead of... | Use... | Why? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| "Predictive Market Volatility" | "Best time to sell your crop" | Clear and benefit-driven. |
| "Soil Composition Analysis" | "Your soil health report" | Humanizes the data. |
| "Government Subsidy Database" | "Help from the Government" | Simplifies the concept. |

- **Greeting:** Use local cultural cues (e.g., "Namaste," "Vanakkam").
- **Urgency:** When a price drop is coming, use clear, non-alarmist warnings: "Market prices may fall tomorrow. Consider selling today."

## 5. Imagery & Photography Guidelines
- **Authenticity:** Avoid "stock" photos of high-tech Western farms. Use imagery of local Indian/regional farms, diverse soil types, and real farmers.
- **Data Visualization:** Use simple bar charts or "Traffic Light" systems (Red/Yellow/Green) for market trends rather than complex candlesticks or line graphs.

## 6. The 3-Layer Architecture in Design
When Antigravity builds the interface, it should visually reflect your architecture:
- **Layer 1 (Directive):** Clear dashboard showing "What to do today."
- **Layer 2 (Orchestration):** The "Why" - showing the weather/price data that led to the advice.
- **Layer 3 (Execution):** The "How" - Direct buttons to "Call Buyer" or "Apply for Scheme."
